## New Bugs

### BUG-1: Friendly fire breaks own team's Polymorph (P2 — AI)

**Frequency:** 2/24 matches (m06, m13)

Allied DPS attacks a target that's Polymorphed by their own team's Mage, breaking the CC early and wasting seconds of crowd control.

**Examples:**
- **m06** (seed 6006): Team 1 Rogue uses Kidney Shot + Sinister Strike on a Warrior that Team 1 Mage Polymorphed. Poly broke at 22.98s (~5s early) from Sinister Strike damage (26 dmg, threshold 0).
  ```
  [ 17.61s] [CC] Team 1 Mage's Polymorph polymorphs Team 2 Warrior (10.0s)
  [ 19.93s] [CAST] Team 1 Rogue uses Kidney Shot on Team 2 Warrior
  [ 22.96s] [DMG] Team 1 Rogue's Sinister Strike hits Team 2 Warrior for 26 damage
  [ 22.98s] [EVENT] Team 2 Warrior's Polymorph broke from damage (26/0)
  ```
- **m13** (seed 6020): Team 1 Warrior Mortal Strikes a Paladin Polymorphed by Team 1 Mage. Poly broke at 22.51s (~5.3s early).
  ```
  [ 17.75s] [CC] Team 1 Mage's Polymorph polymorphs Team 2 Paladin (10.0s)
  [ 22.49s] [DMG] Team 1 Warrior's Mortal Strike hits Team 2 Paladin for 77 damage
  [ 22.51s] [EVENT] Team 2 Paladin's Polymorph broke from damage (77/0)
  ```

**Root cause:** AI target selection doesn't check whether the target has an allied CC (specifically break-on-damage CCs like Polymorph). The DPS AI should skip targets that are Polymorphed/Sapped by their own team.

### BUG-2: Allied damage contributes to breaking own team's Frost Nova (P2 — AI + mechanics)

**Frequency:** 1/24 matches (m11)

Allied Priest wand shot pushes cumulative damage over Frost Nova's break threshold, freeing the enemy the Mage just rooted.

**Example — m11** (seed 6011):
```
[ 19.92s] [CC] Team 1 Mage's Frost Nova roots Team 2 Rogue (6.0s)
[ 20.46s] [DMG] Team 1 Priest's Wand Shot hits Team 2 Rogue for 9 damage
[ 20.46s] [EVENT] Team 2 Rogue's Frost Nova broke from damage (36/35)
```

Frost Nova's own instant damage (27) counted toward the 35 break threshold. The allied Priest's 9-damage wand shot pushed cumulative to 36, breaking the root. Two sub-issues:
1. Frost Nova's own damage probably shouldn't count toward its own break threshold
2. Allied damage shouldn't contribute to breaking allied CC

**Root cause:** Break-on-damage tracking doesn't distinguish damage source (ally vs enemy). In WoW, only enemy damage breaks these CCs.

### BUG-4: Dead combatant events — CC/damage/casts on or from dead targets (P2 — system ordering)

**Frequency:** 3/24 matches (m17, m19, m20)

Same-frame death processing allows events to fire on or from combatants that died in the same tick.

**Examples:**
- **m17** (seed 6024): Mage dies from Ambush and casts Frost Nova in the same frame. The root and damage apply after the Mage's `[DEATH]` entry.
  ```
  [ 18.24s] [CAST] Team 1 Mage casts Frost Nova
  [ 18.24s] [DMG] Team 2 Rogue's Ambush CRITS Team 1 Mage for 183 damage
  [ 18.24s] [DEATH] Team 1 Mage has been eliminated
  [ 18.24s] [DMG] Team 1 Mage's Frost Nova hits Team 2 Rogue for 32 damage
  ```
- **m19** (seed 6026): Dead Warrior receives Polymorph break event, new Polymorph cast targeting, Frostbolt hit, and a second `[DEATH]` entry.
  ```
  [ 19.70s] [DEATH] Team 1 Warrior has been eliminated
  [ 19.72s] [CAST] Team 2 Mage begins casting Polymorph on Team 1 Warrior
  [ 19.72s] [DMG] Team 2 Mage's Frostbolt hits Team 1 Warrior for 94 damage
  [ 19.72s] [DEATH] Team 1 Warrior has been eliminated
  ```
- **m20** (seed 6027): Dead Priest takes a crit auto attack after death in the same frame.

**Root cause:** Death state isn't checked between systems within a single tick. Related to the same-frame ordering issue in BUG-3 but specifically around death cleanup.

### BUG-5: Priest AI never uses Mind Blast in healer-only endgame (P3 — AI)

**Frequency:** 1/24 matches (m10)

When both teams' DPS dies and only healers remain, Priests exclusively wand-trade for 48 seconds without ever attempting Mind Blast.

**Example — m10** (seed 6010):
```
Both Warriors die simultaneously at 35.55s.
From 35.55s to 83.22s: 48 seconds of pure wand trading.
Zero Mind Blast attempts despite available mana (~17+ mana).
Team 2 Priest wins with 2 HP remaining.
```

**Root cause:** Priest AI likely has Mind Blast gated behind conditions (e.g., enemy is DPS class, or specific health thresholds) that don't trigger against a healer-only opponent.

## Balance Observations (Not Bugs)

### Overkill damage display (cosmetic, P3)
In m24, a Frostbolt crit on a 6-HP Warrior displays as "CRITS for 6 damage" instead of the full spell damage. The log shows effective damage (HP remaining) rather than raw spell damage, which is technically correct but reads as confusingly low.

### Healer AI target coordination (P3)
In m20 (3x Priest vs 3x Paladin), all 3 healers consistently cast on the same target in the same frame, producing 2/3 wasted healing (e.g., heals for 80, then 10, then 0). This accelerates OOM and makes multi-healer comps much weaker than they should be.

- **Pillared Arena** didn't produce meaningfully different outcomes from BasicArena in this sample.

### 3v3 Takeaways

- **Mage is the strongest 3v3 class.** 80% win rate, and frequently took 0 damage across entire matches (m21: 676 damage dealt, 0 taken). Frostbolt's combination of damage + CC slow + kiting makes Mage extremely hard to pin down. Mage comps won regardless of partner composition.
- **Warlock remains weak but less terrible than 2v2.** 38% win rate (up from 20% in 2v2). The extra teammate helps protect the Warlock and gives DoTs more time to work. Warlock still died first in 5/8 appearances. In m15, Warlock traded 1-for-1 with Priest via post-death DoT ticks (an acceptable outcome). Warlock's best showing was in m22 (Mage/Wl/Pal won) where the Mage carried.
- **RMP (Rogue/Mage/Priest) is the strongest comp.** Won 3/3 appearances (m14, m18, m24). Lost when matched as the opposing team only when kill targets were set differently (m15, m23). Combines burst (Rogue), control (Mage), and sustain (Priest).
- **Paladin surpasses Priest as healer in 3v3.** Paladin's 78% win rate vs Priest's 56%. Hammer of Justice + Divine Shield + Cleanse utility scales better with more teammates. Paladin comps (WMPal, WRPal, MWlPal, RWlPal) all won.
- **Double healer (m18: War/Priest/Pal)** lost badly — Warrior died in 6.4s under triple focus, then 2 healers had zero kill pressure. Confirms double healer is nonviable.
- **All-same-class matches** produce degenerate games. 3x War vs 3x Mage (m19) was a 21s stomp. 3x Priest vs 3x Pal (m20) was an 83s OOM slugfest.
- **First kill timing** is remarkably consistent: 16-22s across most matches. Whoever dies first almost always determines the winner — comebacks after losing a teammate are extremely rare.
- **Melee cleave (War/Rogue/Pal, m16)** is viable — burst from Ambush crit (270 damage!) plus Warrior pressure can delete a Priest before the opposing team stabilizes.

## Cross-Bracket Summary

### Overall class power rankings:

| Rank | Class | 2v2 WR | 3v3 WR | Notes |
|------|-------|--------|--------|-------|
| 1 | **Mage** | 67% | 80% | Best kiting, damage+CC combo, often takes 0 damage |
| 2 | **Rogue** | 80% | 75% | Explosive opener, small sample in 2v2 |
| 3 | **Priest** | 70% | 56% | Best 2v2 healer, weaker in 3v3 (gets trained) |
| 4 | **Paladin** | 50% | 78% | Weak 2v2 (no offensive tools), excellent 3v3 utility |
| 5 | **Warrior** | 67% | 60% | Strong with healer support, helpless when kited |
| 6 | **Warlock** | 20% | 38% | DoTs get dispelled, dies fast, needs significant help |

### Key balance concerns:
1. **Warlock needs help.** Consistent worst performer in both brackets. DoTs are dispelled instantly, leaving the class with almost no damage contribution.
2. **Mage kiting is too reliable.** Mage frequently takes 0 damage in full matches, suggesting melee gap-closers or anti-kite mechanics need tuning.
3. **First kill is deterministic.** The team that scores the first kill wins ~95% of the time. There's very little comeback potential once a team goes down a member.
4. **Paladin has a stark 2v2/3v3 split.** Weak in 2v2 (no kill pressure when alone with DPS), strong in 3v3 (HoJ/Cleanse/Divine Shield utility shines with more teammates). This is thematically accurate to WoW but worth noting.
